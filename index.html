Mercury:

Distance from Sun: Closest to the Sun (about 36 million miles or 58 million kilometers). Size: Smallest planet in the solar system. Surface: Rocky and heavily cratered.Temperature: Extreme temperature variations due to its lack of atmosphere.

Venus:

Distance from Sun: Second planet from the Sun (about 67 million miles or 108 million kilometers). Size: Similar in size to Earth. Atmosphere: Thick, toxic atmosphere mainly composed of carbon dioxide. Surface: Covered in volcanoes and large impact craters.

Earth:

Distance from Sun: Third planet from the Sun (about 93 million miles or 150 million kilometers). Size: The only known planet to support life; diverse ecosystems. Atmosphere: Oxygen-rich atmosphere suitable for life. Surface: Oceans, landmasses, and a variety of climates.

Mars:

Distance from Sun: Fourth planet from the Sun (about 142 million miles or 228 million kilometers). Size: Smaller than Earth, but larger than Mercury and Venus. Surface: Rusty-red appearance due to iron oxide (rust); evidence of past liquid water. Atmosphere: Thin atmosphere mainly composed of carbon dioxide.

Jupiter:

Distance from Sun: Fifth planet from the Sun (about 484 million miles or 778 million kilometers). Size: Largest planet in the solar system, over 11 times the diameter of Earth. Atmosphere: Thick atmosphere primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Features: Prominent cloud bands, the Great Red Spot (a massive storm), and many moons.

Saturn:

Distance from Sun: Sixth planet from the Sun (about 886 million miles or 1.4 billion kilometers). Size: Second-largest planet; known for its spectacular ring system. Rings: Iconic, extensive ring system made of ice and dust particles. Atmosphere: Primarily hydrogen and helium.
